# city-300 — rung 2 (~300 concurrent) notes

Generated by `scripts/gen-benchmark.sh 300 24 500 900`. See `provenance.txt` for exact
commands/seed/hashes.

## Net-sizing choice (applies to city-300 / city-3000 / city-15000 alike)

Per `BENCHMARK_SPEC.md` "Network generation," option (a) was chosen: **one net, sized at the
15,000-concurrent rung, reused unchanged for every rung** (only demand/period differs). The net is
`netgenerate --grid --grid.number=24 --grid.length=500 -L 1 --tls.guess --seed 42` (24x24 grid,
500 m blocks, single lane per edge per the `-L 1` engine-capability pin already established by
`city-30`'s NOTES.md) — 1,104 lane-km, 576 junctions.

**Why 24x24/500m, empirically (not just a priori density math):** an a priori "jam spacing"
density estimate (bumper-to-bumper ~7.5 m/vehicle) badly overstates what a single-lane,
priority-junction-heavy grid can actually sustain — direct probing (see the session's exploration,
not separately committed) showed several smaller candidate nets (3x3..22x22 at 200-600 m blocks)
either never reaching a genuine steady-state plateau at 15,000 concurrent, or collapsing into
unbounded queueing (SUMO's `running` count growing without bound, `meanSpeedRelative` collapsing
toward 0, `halting`/`running` > 90%) once insertion rate exceeded the network's real throughput
capacity — a macroscopic-fundamental-diagram-style gridlock, not a bug: single-lane signal-free
grids have much lower throughput per lane-km than raw jam-density arithmetic suggests, because
every ~200-600 m block imposes a priority-junction stop/yield. The 24x24/500m net was the first
tried that reached a genuine 15,000-concurrent plateau (`net_capacity_finding=target concurrency
reached within tolerance band`, see `city-15000/provenance.txt`) without collapsing.

**Consequence for city-300 specifically:** reusing this net for the ~300 rung means city-300 is
very SPARSE relative to the shared net (390 mean concurrent / 1,104 lane-km ≈ 0.35 veh/km) — SUMO's
own reference run is essentially free-flow (`meanSpeedRelative=0.98`, `halting=1` at the final
step). This is the accepted tradeoff of the spec's option (a): only demand changes across rungs, so
the smaller rungs necessarily look emptier on the big net. It is NOT itself a finding — the engine
result below is.

## Tuning result

Little's-law tuning converged in ONE iteration: pilot period=40.0s (scaled for this net's size, see
`gen-benchmark.sh`'s `PILOT_PERIOD` formula) -> pilot mean_trip_time=424.65s -> derived
period=1.2448s -> measured mean_running(steady)=390.4, peak_running=485 (target band for
concurrency=300 is [195, 405] -- landed inside it).

## SUMO reference vs engine (VB-6 aggregate comparison) — **FAIL, and why**

| metric | SUMO reference | engine | relError / KS | tol | result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| arrived | 238 | 46 | 0.8067 | 0.35 | **FAIL** |
| mean trip duration (s) | 424.65 | 387.98 | 0.0864 | 0.35 | PASS |
| mean network speed (m/s) | 13.27 | 6.85 | 0.4838 | 0.35 | **FAIL** |
| trip-duration distribution (KS) | -- | -- | 0.1659 | 0.35 | PASS |

**AGGREGATE COMPARISON: FAIL.** This is NOT a demand/net-sizing problem — SUMO runs the identical
net+routes at near-free-flow (`meanSpeedRelative=0.98`). It is a genuine **engine correctness
defect**, root-caused and written up in full at
`/NEED-priorityjunction-farrouted-foe-falsepositive.md` (repo root): `Engine.FindFoeVehicle`
(`src/Sim.Core/Engine.cs:2279`) matches a candidate "foe" vehicle for a priority-junction minor
link by checking whether the foe's internal lane appears **anywhere in the foe's full route-long
lane sequence**, with NO proximity/time-window filter -- so on a large, busy network, essentially
every minor-link approach finds SOME vehicle somewhere in the city whose eventual route crosses
that lane, and treats it as an imminently-approaching foe forever. The engine reports **425 of 723
departed vehicles (58.8%) EVER stuck** (>=120s continuously below 0.1 m/s) and **404 still stuck at
the end of the run** — traced to specific vehicles parked exactly at a minor-link stop line for
400+ continuous seconds while SUMO shows near-zero halting on the same input. `city-30`'s bring-up
(9 junctions, ~40 concurrent) never triggered this because at that tiny scale "some vehicle's route
crosses this lane" and "some vehicle is actually near this junction" are nearly the same fact; the
576-junction net exposes the gap.

Per `CLAUDE.md`, benchmark work does not modify `Sim.Core` — this is reported, not patched, here.

## Engine performance

900 steps in 28.78 s wall time (RTF 31.27x -- far below `city-30`'s 1300-2300x, both because the
net has 576 junctions vs. 9 (more per-step junction-conflict bookkeeping regardless of vehicle
count) and because `FindFoeVehicle`'s full-route scan is effectively O(active vehicles) per
foe-check, compounded by the stuck vehicles inflating the engine's own resident count well above
SUMO's -- peak concurrent 678 (engine) vs. 485 (SUMO) on the identical demand, since arrivals stall
while insertions continue). Peak RSS 159.1 MiB, 325.8 MiB total allocation.

## Viz

`replay.html` is built from the **SUMO reference trajectory** (`sumo -c config.sumocfg
--fcd-output ...`, not committed — regenerate on demand), not the engine's own FCD. Two reasons:
(1) the engine's `engine.fcd.xml` at this rung is ~43 MB (dominated by ~400 vehicles parked
motionless for hundreds of seconds — not "watchable," and far too large to keep "small enough to
commit" per `BENCHMARK_SPEC.md`'s rung-2 guidance); (2) the SUMO reference gives a clean,
representative view of the intended free-flowing scenario. The stuck-vehicle defect itself is
better inspected numerically (`engine.tripinfo.xml` / the stuck-count above) than visually at this
scale. `replay.html` is ~16.8 MB (larger than `city-30`'s 1.2 MB) because it embeds the FULL shared
24x24/1,104-lane-km network geometry (12,952 lanes, 576 junctions) regardless of how sparse the
300-concurrent demand is on it -- the accepted cost of the option-(a) shared-net choice.
